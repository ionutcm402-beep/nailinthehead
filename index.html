NEVER USED AI BEFORE?
Start here.

This page is for complete beginners. No jargon. No assumptions. Just what you need to know to get started — in plain English.

Step 1 — What is AI, actually?

AI tools like ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini are like having a very well-read assistant you can type questions to. You type something — it types back. That's it. No app to download. No account needed to start. And you can't break anything.

The reason most people get rubbish answers is simple — they don't give it enough detail. "Write me a complaint letter" gets a generic, useless result. "Write me a formal complaint letter to British Gas about an incorrect bill of £340 for a flat I moved out of 3 months ago" gets something you can actually use.

The golden rule: The more specific you are, the better the answer. That's what our prompts do for you — they ask the right questions in the right way.

Step 2 — Which AI tool should I use?

All of these are free to use and work with our prompts:

ChatGPT

The most well-known. Free version works great for our prompts.

chat.openai.com →
Claude

Great for letters and longer pieces. Very natural to talk to.

claude.ai →
Gemini

Google's AI. Good if you already use Google services.

gemini.google.com →

All three require a free account. Just sign up with your email — it takes 2 minutes.

Step 3 — Your first prompt

Here's exactly what to do:

  1. Go to one of the AI tools above and sign in
  2. Come back here and find a prompt that matches something you need to do
  3. Click Copy prompt
  4. Paste it into the AI tool (Ctrl+V or tap and hold → Paste)
  5. Fill in the bits in [SQUARE BRACKETS] with your own details
  6. Press Enter or click Send
  7. Read what comes back — if it's not quite right, just say "make it shorter" or "make it more formal" and it will adjust

Don't worry about getting it perfect first time. AI is a conversation — you can keep refining the answer until it's exactly what you need.

Step 4 — Common questions

Is it safe to use?

Yes — but don't share sensitive personal details like your full bank account number, passwords, or National Insurance number. Give the AI enough context to help you without sharing anything you wouldn't tell a stranger.

Will it always give me the right answer?

No. AI can get things wrong — especially dates, specific laws, or recent events. Always read what it gives you before using it. For anything important (legal, medical, financial) treat it as a starting point, not the final answer.

Do I need to pay?

No. All the AI tools mentioned above have a free version that works perfectly for our prompts. This site is also completely free.

What if I get a weird or wrong answer?

Just tell it. Type "that's not quite right, try again" or "make it more professional" or "shorter please". AI responds to plain English instructions — you don't need special commands.

Ready to try it?

Pick a prompt, copy it, and paste it into any free AI tool. You'll be surprised how easy it is.

LESSON 1 OF 5
Why AI probably confused you the first time

If you tried AI and got a rubbish answer — that wasn't your fault. It wasn't even really AI's fault. It's just that nobody told you the one thing you need to know to get a good result.

AI is not a search engine. When you type something into Google, you're looking for a page that already exists. When you type something into AI, you're having a conversation. And like any conversation — the more context you give, the better the response.

Most people type something like: "Write me a complaint letter."

And they get back something generic and useless. Of course they do — the AI has no idea who you are, what happened, who you're complaining to, or what you want as an outcome.

The golden rule: Give AI the same detail you'd give a solicitor, a doctor, or a very thorough friend. The more specific you are, the better the answer.

That's what our prompts do. They ask AI the right questions, in the right way, with space for your specific details. Instead of "write me a complaint letter", a good prompt says exactly who you are, what happened, when, what you want back, and what tone to use.

The result? Something you can actually use.

Try this now: Go to ChatGPT or Claude (both free). Type: "Write me a complaint letter to British Gas about an overcharge of £200 on my account. I am a domestic customer and I want a full refund and an apology." See the difference a bit of detail makes.

LESSON 2 OF 5
How to talk to AI so it actually understands you

Now you know AI needs detail — let's talk about how to structure what you say. There's a simple formula that works every time.

1. Tell it what you want

Be specific. Not "write a letter" — "write a formal complaint letter" or "write a friendly but firm email".

2. Give it context

Who are you? What happened? When? Who is it to? The more background you give, the more relevant the answer.

3. Tell it the outcome you want

Do you want a refund? An apology? A job interview? A payment plan? Say it explicitly.

4. Tell it the tone

Professional? Friendly? Firm? Apologetic? AI will match whatever tone you ask for.

5. Tell it the format

"Under 200 words" / "bullet points" / "formal letter format" / "conversational". Format matters.

You don't need to use all five every time. But the more of these you include, the better your result will be.

Remember:

AI is a conversation. If the first answer isn't right, just say so. "Make it shorter", "more formal please", "try again but angrier" — it all works. You're not stuck with the first answer.

LESSON 3 OF 5
Getting AI to write letters and emails for you

This is where AI saves most people the most time. Letters, emails, formal complaints, cover letters, references — all of it.

Most people hate writing formal documents. They don't know the right language. They worry about getting the tone wrong. They spend an hour on something that should take 10 minutes.

AI removes all of that. Here's a real example:

WHAT YOU TYPE INTO AI:

Write me a formal complaint letter to my landlord about a boiler that has been broken for 3 weeks. I have reported it twice by phone and once by email but nothing has been done. It is now winter and I have no heating or hot water. I want it fixed within 48 hours or I will contact the council. My name is [NAME], the property address is [ADDRESS]. Professional tone, under 200 words.

What you get back: A properly formatted, legally aware complaint letter that references your right to heating, gives a clear deadline, and sounds like it was written by someone who knows what they're doing. In about 10 seconds.

Our prompt library has 210 prompts already written for exactly these situations. You don't even need to figure out what to type — just find your situation, fill in your details, and paste.

Pro tip: After AI writes your letter, ask it: "What am I missing? Is there anything I should add to make this stronger?" It will often suggest things you hadn't thought of.

LESSON 4 OF 5
Using AI for money, benefits and dealing with companies

This is where AI can genuinely change your financial situation. Most people don't know their rights. Companies count on that.

Benefits you might be missing

Ask AI: "I am [your situation]. What UK benefits might I be entitled to that I'm not currently claiming?" Around £19 billion in benefits goes unclaimed every year. AI knows the system.

Challenging unfair charges

Bank fees, energy bills, parking fines — AI can write the challenge letter and tell you your legal rights. Most people never challenge anything because they don't know how. Now you do.

Understanding documents

Got a confusing letter from HMRC, a solicitor, or your mortgage provider? Paste it into AI and ask: "Explain this in plain English. What do I need to do and by when?"

Negotiating better deals

Ask AI for a script to call your energy supplier, broadband provider, or mobile company. It will tell you exactly what to say, what to ask for, and what to do if they say no.

Important: Always tell AI you are in the UK. Laws, rights, and processes are different here. A prompt written for the US won't reference the Consumer Rights Act or the Financial Ombudsman. Always specify.

LESSON 5 OF 5
Making AI work for you every single week

You've made it to the last lesson. Here's how to make AI a habit that actually saves you time and money.

The people who get the most out of AI aren't the most tech-savvy. They're the ones who remember to use it. Here's a simple weekly habit:

MON

Any admin or letters you've been putting off — do them with AI in 20 minutes instead of worrying about them all week.

WED

Any question you've been meaning to look up — ask AI instead of Googling. You'll get a direct answer, not 10 links.

FRI

Plan something for next week — a budget, a meal plan, a work task — using AI to help structure it.

That's it. Three small moments a week. Within a month it becomes second nature.
