The Story

You were never bad at your job.

Most people who feel overwhelmed at work are not overwhelmed by the important parts. They're buried by the small stuff — the emails that need to be just right, the documents that take forever to start, the meetings that needed preparing for.

These tasks aren't hard because you're not good enough. They're hard because they require a particular kind of writing and thinking that drains everyone — the kind where every word matters and getting it wrong has consequences.

AI doesn't replace your work. It removes the friction around it. It drafts, so you edit. It plans, so you decide. It researches, so you act. The thinking stays yours. The labour is shared.

How It Works

The path
forward.

You don't need to understand AI to use it well. You just need to know what to ask — and when. Here's the path.

01
Start with the situation, not the task
Don't just ask AI to "write an email." Tell it the full situation — who's involved, what's at stake, what you're trying to achieve, and what tone you want. The more context you give, the more useful the output.
02
Give it your voice
Paste in an example of how you usually write — another email, a message you're proud of. Tell AI: "Match this tone." Your voice stays yours. AI just does the heavy lifting.
03
Use it to prepare, not just produce
Before any difficult meeting or conversation, ask AI to help you prepare. What should you say? What might they say back? What are you missing? Preparation is the most underused superpower in professional life.
04
Let it research for you
Need to understand a topic, a person, a company, or a situation before a meeting? AI can give you a thorough, organised overview in minutes. Walk in knowing more than anyone else in the room.
05
Edit, don't accept
AI gives you a first draft. Your job is to make it yours. Read it. Change what doesn't sound like you. Add the thing only you know. What takes thirty minutes now takes five — and it's better.

Prompt · Email Writing
Write this professional email for me
For any email that matters — the ones you keep putting off because the stakes are too high.
I need to write a professional email to [who] about [situation]. The context is: [explain briefly]. I want to come across as [confident/warm/firm/professional — choose one]. Keep it under 200 words. Make it clear, polite, and impossible to misunderstand.
✓ Sent it. Got a reply within an hour. Never would have got that reply from what I'd written myself.
Prompt · Pay Rise
Ask for a salary increase without sounding desperate
One of the most important conversations most people never have. This makes it easier.
Help me write a professional email asking for a pay rise. I have been in my current role for [X]. My key achievements are: [list 3-4 things]. The industry rate for this role is approximately [£X]. I want to be confident but not aggressive. Make it clear I have earned this.
✓ Got the meeting. Got the increase. Best prompt I've ever used.
Prompt · Difficult Conversations
Prepare me for this hard conversation
For the conversations you've been putting off. The feedback you need to give. The boundaries you need to set.
I need to have a difficult conversation with [person — colleague/manager/client] about [issue]. I want to: [your goal]. I'm worried about: [what might go wrong]. Give me: the key things I need to say, how to start the conversation, and what to do if they react badly.
✓ Went in calm. Said everything I needed to say. Nothing went wrong.
Prompt · Meeting Prep
Make me the most prepared person in the room
Before any meeting where you need to impress, influence, or simply not be caught off guard.
I have a meeting with [who] about [topic] on [when]. I want to: [your goal for the meeting]. Help me prepare: what should I say, what questions should I ask, what objections might come up and how should I handle them, and what should I know about the other person's likely priorities?
✓ Walked in knowing exactly what was going to happen. And exactly what to say.
Prompt · Document Writing
Turn my scattered notes into a clear document
For every report, proposal, plan, or document you've been putting off starting.
Here are my rough notes on [topic]: [paste your notes or bullet points — as messy as you like]. Please turn these into a clear, professional document. Structure it properly. Make the writing clean and confident. Keep my key points but make them flow.
✓ A document I'd been avoiding for a week. Done in 20 minutes.
Prompt · Research
Understand anything before you need to act on it
Before a pitch, a negotiation, a client meeting, or any situation where knowledge is power.
I need to understand [topic/company/person/situation] quickly and thoroughly. Give me: the most important things to know, what most people get wrong about it, and three smart questions I could ask about it that would make me look informed and thoughtful.
✓ Walked into a pitch knowing more than the client expected. Won the work.
Common Questions

Everything
you wondered.

Will it sound like me or like a robot?+
If you give it examples of how you write and tell it the tone you want, it will sound like you — just a better version of you. Always read it and adjust anything that doesn't feel right. The goal is to start in the right place, not to use the first draft unchanged.
What if I work in a formal or regulated industry?+
AI adapts to the register you ask for. Tell it: "This is a formal legal/medical/financial context" and it will match that. Always review the output carefully — you know your industry, AI provides the draft.
I feel like I'm cheating if I use AI at work. Is that normal?+
Very normal — and worth reconsidering. You don't feel like you're cheating for using spell check, or for using a calculator, or for using Google. AI is a tool. What matters is the quality of your thinking, your decisions, and your judgement — and AI leaves all of that to you.
Can it help me with job applications too?+
Yes. Significantly. CV rewriting, cover letters, interview preparation, salary negotiation — all of it. The Earn More room goes deeper into this specifically.
What if my company doesn't allow AI use?+
Check your company's policy. Many are fine with AI for drafting as long as you review and own the final output. If in doubt, ask. If it's genuinely restricted, the thinking frameworks on this page still apply — you just do the writing yourself.
