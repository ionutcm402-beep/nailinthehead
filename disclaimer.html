About AI tools

AI tools are powerful.
They are also imperfect.

The AI tools referenced on this website — including ChatGPT, Claude, Google Gemini, and others — are remarkable, useful, and genuinely helpful for many tasks. They are also capable of making mistakes, producing inaccurate information, and occasionally generating content that sounds authoritative but is wrong.

This is sometimes called "hallucination" — the tendency of AI tools to produce plausible-sounding but factually incorrect information. It happens. It happens less than it used to, and it will happen less in future. But for now, it happens.

What this means in practice

Use AI as a thinking partner, not as an oracle. The prompts on this website are designed to help you think, explore, and generate ideas — not to produce final answers you should act on without further verification.

Before making an important decision based on something an AI tool told you — especially in areas of finance, health, law, or any other high-stakes field — please verify the information with a reliable, qualified source.

AI tools do not know your personal situation. They do not know your financial position, your legal jurisdiction, your health history, or the specific context of your life. Always apply your own judgement, and consult professionals where appropriate.

The prompts on this website are designed to help you have better conversations, think more clearly, and explore possibilities. They are a starting point, not a final word.
