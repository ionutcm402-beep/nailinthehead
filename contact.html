personally.

While I may not be able to reply to every email, I genuinely appreciate hearing from people who are exploring AI and trying to build a better future for themselves.

Your questions help me understand what to write next. Your feedback makes the site better. Your stories — the things that actually happened when you tried these prompts — are the most motivating thing I receive.

There is no support team here. It is just me, reading your messages when I have time. So please be patient if a reply takes a while. I am not ignoring you — I am working on making this better.

What I love to hear about

The things worth
writing about.

❓
01
Questions

Something you do not understand. A prompt that did not work the way you expected. A situation you are trying to navigate that the site does not cover yet.

Send it. Genuinely. Questions like this are often the starting point for new prompts, new rooms, and new articles. If you are wondering, chances are a thousand other people are wondering the same thing and not asking.

💬
02
Feedback

Something that could be clearer. A section that felt confusing. A prompt that worked better than expected — or worse. A page that felt cold, or warm, or exactly right.

All of it is useful. This site is a work in progress. The feedback of people actually using it is far more valuable than any analysis I can do on my own.

✔
03
Corrections

Something that is wrong. A fact that is out of date. A prompt that produces unhelpful results in a specific AI tool. A claim that does not hold up.

Please tell me. This site is built on the principle of honesty, and that includes being honest when something here is incorrect. I would rather be corrected than be wrong.

🌟
04
Your Story

Something that happened when you used one of these prompts. A decision you made differently. Something you understood that had always confused you. A thing you built, or started, or finally wrote.

These are the messages I love most. They are the reason this site exists. They are the proof — more than any analytics, more than any metric — that any of this is worth doing.

No pressure. Ever.

Just say
hello.

You do not need a big question or an important reason. You can just say hello. I built this site because I wanted to help people, and hearing from people who are using it — whether it helped, whether it did not, whether they are still figuring it out — is genuinely one of the best parts of having built it.
