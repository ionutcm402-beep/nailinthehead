The Story

The ideas were always there.

Creativity doesn't die in adulthood. It gets buried — under the weight of responsibility, practicality, time, and the quiet fear that your ideas aren't good enough. Most people stop creating long before they run out of things to create.

The biggest lie about creativity is that it's a talent you either have or you don't. It isn't. It's a practice — and like all practices, it gets easier with the right support. AI is the support that has been missing.

AI doesn't create for you. It creates alongside you. It helps you start when the blank page is terrifying. It helps you develop an idea when it's still shapeless. It helps you finish when you run out of momentum. The voice, the vision, the meaning — those are yours.

How It Works

The path
forward.

You don't need to understand AI to use it well. You just need to know what to ask — and when.

01
Give yourself permission to begin badly
The biggest block is perfectionism. AI removes it. You don't need a perfect first draft — you need any first draft. Tell AI your rough idea and ask it to help you begin, not to produce something finished.
02
Think out loud with AI
Your best ideas are often buried under the clutter of half-thoughts. Use AI as a thinking partner. Describe your idea loosely and ask it to help you see the shape of it. What's really interesting here? What's the angle?
03
Separate creation from editing
Create first. Edit second. Never at the same time. Use AI to generate momentum in the creation phase — don't let it make you more critical of yourself earlier.
04
Use it to break through blocks
When you're stuck, describe where you're stuck to AI. 'I have a section that needs to do X but I can't figure out how to get there.' It will offer paths. You choose the one that feels right.
05
Protect your voice
Always read what AI produces and ask: does this sound like me? Rewrite anything that doesn't. Use AI for momentum and structure — use yourself for character and voice.

Prompt · The First Line
Help me start the thing I keep not starting
The most important prompt in this room. The beginning of everything.
I have been wanting to create [describe what you want to make — a book, a blog, a script, a business, a poem, anything]. I keep not starting because [be honest about why]. Help me write just the very beginning — not the whole thing, just the first few lines or the opening — in a way that captures the spirit of what I want to make and makes me want to continue.
✓ Finally started the novel I've been planning for six years. Chapter one exists. The rest will follow.
Prompt · Idea Development
Take my rough idea and make it real
When you have a shape in your head but you can't quite see the full picture.
I have an idea for [describe it — as rough and incomplete as it feels]. What I'm trying to say or create is something like: [describe the feeling or meaning, not just the content]. Help me: understand what's most interesting about this idea, find the right structure or form for it, and identify what would make it genuinely worth making.
✓ My idea was a mess. It came out the other side with a structure I could actually build.
Prompt · Beat the Block
I am stuck — help me find the way through
For the moment in the middle of any project when forward motion stops.
I am working on [describe your project]. I'm stuck at [describe exactly where and why]. What I'm trying to achieve at this point is [explain it]. Give me three different ways I could approach this. I'll choose — I just need to see the options.
✓ Was stuck for two weeks. Took ten minutes. Back in motion.
Prompt · Find Your Voice
Write this in a way that sounds like me
Because the greatest risk with AI and creativity is that everything starts sounding the same.
Here are three examples of my writing that I'm happy with: [paste them]. Now help me write [the new thing you're creating] in the same voice. Same rhythm, same tone, same personality. If you see me drifting from this voice in future drafts, tell me.
✓ Read it back and it actually sounded like me. That was the moment I started trusting the process.
Prompt · The Pitch
Explain what I am making so compellingly that I want to make it
Sometimes you need to sell the idea to yourself before you can build it.
I am creating [describe your project]. Help me write a short, compelling description of it — the kind of thing you'd tell someone at a dinner party that makes them say 'I want to read or see or hear that.' This isn't for marketing yet — it's for me, to remind me what I'm building and why it matters.
✓ Sent it to a friend. She said: please make this. That was all the permission I needed.
Prompt · Finish What You Started
Help me complete something I abandoned
For the creative work that exists in a drawer, a folder, or your head — unfinished.
I started [describe the project] but didn't finish it. Here is what I have so far: [describe or paste what exists]. Here is why I stopped: [be honest]. Help me make a simple plan to finish this — in the smallest possible steps, so the momentum builds rather than overwhelms.
✓ Finished a short story I'd abandoned three years ago. It turned out to be the best thing I've ever written.
Common Questions

Everything
you wondered.

Am I cheating if AI helps me create something?+
No more than you're cheating by using a word processor instead of a typewriter, or by asking a friend to read your draft. Tools have always been part of creative work. What matters is the vision, the voice, the meaning — and those belong entirely to you.
Will my work end up sounding like everyone else's?+
Only if you let it. The Voice prompt above addresses this directly. The key is to treat AI output as raw material, not finished product. Your job is to read it, reshape it, and make it yours.
I've never thought of myself as a creative person. Is this room for me?+
Especially for you. The belief that creativity belongs to other people is one of the most costly myths most people carry. If you have things you want to say, express, make, or build — you are creative.
What kinds of creative work can AI help with?+
Writing of all kinds. Business ideas. Content creation. Script writing. Course creation. Blog posts. Social media. Presentations. Pitches. Personal projects. If you're trying to make something, AI can help in some part of the process.
What if the AI suggestions are terrible?+
Ignore them and ask again. Or use them as a jumping-off point for your own thinking. Bad suggestions are sometimes more useful than no suggestions — they tell you what the thing isn't, which helps you understand what it is.
