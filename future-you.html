The Story

The best version of you isn't a dream.

Most people have a vague sense of who they want to become. They can feel it — the clearer thinking, the better career, the more creative life, the more intentional relationships. But the gap between feeling it and building toward it has always been enormous.

The problem is not ambition. The problem is that personal transformation has always required either an expensive coach, the right environment, or an unusual amount of self-awareness and self-discipline. Most people have none of these in sufficient quantity.

AI changes the economics of personal growth entirely. For the first time, anyone can have a thinking partner who helps them design their life intentionally — not with vague inspiration, but with specific plans, honest challenge, and a working architecture for change.

How It Works

The path
forward.

You don't need to understand AI to use it well. You just need to know what to ask.

01
Start with who you are right now
Honest self-knowledge is the foundation of everything. Before you describe the future, describe the present. Not as self-criticism — as inventory. What you're good at, what holds you back, what you've built, what you've left undone.
02
Describe who you want to be — in detail
Not 'more successful.' Not 'happier.' Specific. What are you doing in three years? What does your work look like? Your relationships? Your daily rhythm? The more specific the picture, the more real it becomes.
03
Find the gap honestly
The distance between who you are now and who you want to become is made of specific things. Habits you don't have. Skills you haven't built. Decisions you keep avoiding. AI helps you name them precisely.
04
Build the smallest possible plan
Transformation fails when the steps are too big. Build a 90-day plan of the smallest possible actions — things you can actually do in your actual life, with the time and energy you actually have.
05
Come back and recalibrate
The person you're becoming is always changing. Use AI as a regular thinking partner — monthly, weekly, whenever you need clarity. Transformation is not a one-time event. It is a continuous conversation with yourself.

Prompt · The Future Portrait
Write a description of who I am becoming
The most powerful prompt on this page. Possibly on this entire site.
I want to describe two versions of myself. Here is who I am today: [describe your current self honestly — your work, your habits, your relationships, your strengths, your struggles]. Here is who I want to be in three years: [describe it as specifically as you can]. Please write me a vivid, specific, honest portrait of Future Me — written as if that person already exists. Not a fantasy. A realistic, earned version of my best self.
✓ Read it three times. Felt the distance between now and then collapse. Made one phone call that afternoon I'd been putting off for months.
Prompt · The Gap Map
Show me exactly what stands between who I am and who I want to be
For the honest reckoning that makes change possible.
I want to become [describe your goal or the version of yourself you're working toward]. I am currently [describe where you are honestly]. Please identify: the three to five most important things that actually separate these two versions of me, which of these I can change and which I cannot, and what the most important first move would be if I were serious about this.
✓ None of it was what I expected. All of it was true. That combination is rare and valuable.
Prompt · The 90-Day Plan
Build me a real plan I can actually follow
Not a vision board. Not a list of aspirations. A working architecture for the next 90 days.
My goal for the next 90 days is to [describe it specifically]. My current situation is: [describe what you have to work with — time, energy, resources, constraints]. Please build me a 90-day plan with: weekly focus areas, specific daily or weekly actions that are small enough to actually do, how to measure whether I'm on track, and how to reset if I fall off.
✓ First time I've had a plan that felt designed for my real life rather than borrowed from someone else's.
Prompt · The Habit Design
Build me habits that fit my actual life
For the habits you want but have never managed to make stick.
I want to build the following habit: [describe it]. My realistic daily schedule looks like: [describe it honestly]. My biggest obstacles to sticking with habits are: [be honest]. Design a habit system for me that: fits into my real schedule without requiring willpower I don't have, includes a specific trigger and reward, and has a simple recovery plan for when I miss a day.
✓ This is the first habit system I've stuck with for more than two weeks.
Prompt · The Letter
Write me a letter from my future self
For perspective. For motivation. For the times when you need to remember why you started.
My goal is to [describe it]. The version of me who has achieved this goal would be [describe Future You briefly]. Please write me a letter from that future version of myself to where I am today. It should: acknowledge the difficulty of what I'm trying to do, give me specific encouragement based on what you know about my situation, and remind me of what I'm building toward in a way that makes me want to continue.
✓ I read it when I want to give up. It works every time.
Prompt · The Monthly Check-in
Help me stay on track and recalibrate
For regular use — monthly, or whenever you need to reset.
Here is the goal I set [X weeks/months ago]: [describe it]. Here is where I am today: [describe your honest progress]. Here is what has worked: [list it]. Here is what has not worked: [list it honestly]. Please help me: understand what my progress tells me about what I need to do differently, adjust my plan for the next 30 days, and identify the one most important thing to focus on.
✓ Monthly recalibration has been more valuable than the original plan. This prompt is part of my routine now.
Common Questions

Everything
you wondered.

Is this the same as life coaching?+
AI can do many of the things a life coach does — helping you clarify goals, challenge assumptions, build plans, and stay accountable. What it can't do is build a human relationship, hold genuine emotional space, or provide the accountability that comes from a real person who cares about you. For many people, AI is a powerful starting point. For some, it's all they need. For others, it opens the door to seeking the human support they actually need.
What if I don't know what I want?+
Start there. Use AI to help you figure it out. The Clarity prompt from the Think Better room is a good starting point. Tell AI honestly that you're not sure what you want and ask it to help you find out through questions.
This feels quite personal. Is it safe to share things with AI?+
Don't share anything you'd be uncomfortable with, including identifying information, financial details, or private information about others. The prompts here are designed to work with general descriptions. You don't need to give AI your name, your address, or your bank account to have a meaningful conversation about your goals.
I've tried goal-setting before and it doesn't work for me.+
Most goal-setting fails because the goals are too vague, the plans too big, and there's no mechanism for recovery when you fall off. The 90-Day Plan and Habit Design prompts above are specifically built around the reasons goal-setting fails. The key is starting smaller than you think you need to.
How often should I use these prompts?+
The Future Portrait is worth doing once a quarter. The Monthly Check-in is worth doing monthly. The others you can use whenever you need them. There's no correct frequency — the right amount is the amount that keeps you moving.
