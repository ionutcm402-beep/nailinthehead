How It Works

The path
forward.

You don't need to understand AI to use it well. You just need to know what to ask — and when. Here's the path.

01
Tell it exactly where you are
Don't ask general questions. Tell AI your starting point. "I know nothing about X. Explain it to me simply, with a real example." The more honest you are about your level, the better it teaches.
02
Ask for the thing behind the thing
Most confusion comes from missing one foundational idea. Ask AI: "What's the one concept I need to understand before everything else makes sense?" Then understand that one thing completely before moving on.
03
Make it concrete to your life
Abstract ideas don't stick. Ask AI to give you an example from your own world — your job, your family, your situation. The moment a concept connects to something real, it stays forever.
04
Test yourself out loud
After AI explains something, try explaining it back in your own words. Ask AI to listen and tell you where you're still fuzzy. This is how understanding becomes knowledge.
05
Follow the thread
Learning compounds. One understood idea opens the door to the next. Ask AI "what should I understand next, given what I just learned?" and let the path build itself.
Your Prompts

These prompts are ready to use right now. Copy any one, paste it into ChatGPT, Claude, or any AI tool, fill in the brackets — and go.

Prompt · Understand Anything
Explain it simply — with my life as the example
Use this when you finally want to understand something that's always confused you.
I want to understand [topic or concept]. Explain it to me as if I am an intelligent adult who knows absolutely nothing about it. Use one simple metaphor and one example from everyday life — not from textbooks. Then ask me if I have questions.
✓ Used this for tax returns. Finally understood it after 15 years of guessing.
Prompt · Find the Foundation
What do I need to know first?
Every subject has one thing that unlocks the rest. Find it here.
I want to learn about [subject]. Before I go any further, what is the single most important foundational idea I need to understand? Explain just that one thing — clearly, simply, and completely. Nothing else yet.
✓ Spent years learning the wrong things first. This changed how I approach everything.
Prompt · Skill Sprint
Teach me the 20% that gives 80% of results
When you need to get good at something fast — and you need to focus on what actually matters.
I need to learn [skill] as quickly as possible. I don't need to be an expert — I need to be competent and confident. What are the 5 most important things to understand or practise? Ignore everything that isn't essential.
✓ Learned the basics of Excel in one afternoon. Could have saved years of struggle.
Prompt · Study Partner
Quiz me until I actually know this
The best way to learn is to be tested. This turns AI into your personal examiner.
I've just read/learnt about [topic]. Here's what I think I know: [summarise it in your own words]. Now ask me 5 questions that test whether I really understand it — not just facts, but the meaning behind them. Tell me where I'm still unclear.
✓ Preparing for my professional exam. This method is better than every revision guide I've tried.
Prompt · The Gap Finder
What am I misunderstanding?
Sometimes you think you understand something but you don't quite. This finds the gap.
I believe I understand [concept]. My understanding is: [explain it in your own words]. Where am I wrong, oversimplifying, or missing something important? Be direct — I'd rather know now than discover it later.
✓ Found a misunderstanding I'd carried for ten years. Embarrassing but essential.
Prompt · Make It Stick
Help me remember this forever
Understanding is one thing. Remembering it is another. This helps the second part.
I've just understood [concept or fact]. Help me remember it long-term. Give me: one memorable analogy, one unusual real-world application, and one question I can ask myself in a week to check I still know it.
✓ I finally understand things AND remember them. Two for the price of one.
Common Questions

Everything
you wondered.

Do I need to know anything about AI to use this?+
Not at all. You just type as if you're talking to a very knowledgeable friend. The prompts on this page are designed so you can copy them exactly, change the words in brackets, and go.
Which AI tool should I use?+
ChatGPT (free at chat.openai.com), Claude (free at claude.ai), or Google Gemini (free at gemini.google.com) — all work perfectly. You don't need to pay for anything to get started.
What if the explanation is still confusing?+
Just say so. Type "That's still confusing — explain it differently, even more simply." AI will try again. And again. It never gets impatient, and it never makes you feel stupid for asking.
Can I actually learn complex things this way?+
Yes. In fact, complex things often become easier with AI because you can ask exactly the questions you're too embarrassed to ask a real teacher. The absence of judgement changes everything.
How long does it take to learn something new?+
Depends on the subject. Some things — like understanding a concept you've been confused about — take minutes. A new skill might take a few focused sessions. But almost everything goes faster than you expect.
