The Story

You've always been worth more.

Most people who are underpaid are not underskilled. They are underrepresented — including to themselves. They struggle to articulate their own value, to communicate it persuasively, to walk into a conversation with the confidence that comes from knowing what they're worth.

AI changes this in a very specific way. It holds up a mirror to your experience and reflects it back in language that is clear, compelling, and impossible to argue with. It sees what you've done and says — clearly, plainly — why it matters.

This is not about gaming the system. It is about finally being seen accurately. Your skills are real. Your experience is real. AI helps you communicate them at the level they deserve.

How It Works

The path
forward.

You don't need to understand AI to use it well. You just need to know what to ask — and when.

01
Inventory everything you've done
Most people massively underestimate their own experience. Use AI to do a full inventory — every role, every achievement, every skill. Not as a CV exercise. As a genuine accounting of what you've built.
02
Find the language for your value
The difference between 'I manage a team' and 'I lead a team of eight people responsible for £2.4m of annual revenue' is the same experience — described differently. AI helps you find the second version.
03
Research what you should actually be earning
Before any salary conversation, know the market. Ask AI to help you understand typical rates for your role, your experience, and your location. Walk in with evidence, not just a feeling.
04
Prepare for every possible response
What if they say no? What if they ask why? Prepare a response to everything. AI can run through the whole conversation so you're never caught off guard.
05
Build new income pathways
Employed income is one stream. But your skills almost certainly have value beyond your job. AI can help you see the freelance, consultancy, or product opportunities your experience enables.
Your Prompts

These prompts are ready to use right now. Copy any one, paste it into ChatGPT, Claude, or any AI tool, fill in the brackets — and go.

Prompt · Value Mirror
Show me what I am actually worth
For the moment before you walk into any salary, rate, or fee conversation. Know yourself first.
Here is my professional background: [describe your role, years of experience, key skills and achievements]. Write me a compelling, honest paragraph that makes the strongest possible case for my professional value — the kind of thing I'd struggle to say about myself but that is completely true.
✓ Read it back and thought: I didn't know I was this good. Used it as the opening to my pay rise conversation.
Prompt · Pay Rise Email
Ask for a salary increase with confidence
The email most people never send because they do not know how to start it.
I want to ask my manager for a pay rise. I have been in my current role for [X]. My key achievements include: [list 3 specific things — with numbers where possible]. The market rate for this role in my area is approximately [amount]. Write me a professional, confident email making this request. Not aggressive — assured.
✓ Sent it on a Tuesday. Had a meeting booked by Thursday. Got the increase the following month.
Prompt · Salary Negotiation
Give me a script I can actually use
For when you have the meeting — and you need to know exactly what to say.
I have been offered a salary of [X]. I want [Y] based on [my experience / market rate / my achievements]. Help me prepare for this negotiation. Give me: what to say to open the conversation, how to respond if they push back, how to respond if they say the budget is not available, and how to close confidently.
✓ Negotiated above the offer. Every objection they raised, I had an answer.
Prompt · Freelance Pitch
Win the work you want
For freelancers, contractors, and anyone who needs to pitch their skills.
I am a [your profession] with [X years experience]. I specialise in [your speciality]. I am pitching for a project that involves: [describe the brief]. Write me a professional pitch that: demonstrates I understand their problem, shows why I am the right person, includes relevant experience, and ends with a clear next step.
✓ Won the project. Client said my pitch was the clearest and most confident they received.
Prompt · CV Rewrite
Make my CV impossible to ignore
Before any job application. This is the most important document in your professional life.
Here is my current CV: [paste it or describe your experience]. Rewrite it so that: every achievement uses strong, specific language with numbers where possible, the opening summary makes an immediate case for why I am valuable, and the whole thing reads as a story of growth and impact — not just a list of jobs.
✓ Three interviews from the next four applications. The CV did the work I could not do myself.
Prompt · New Income Streams
Find the money hiding in your skills
For anyone who suspects their skills are worth more than one employer's salary.
My professional background is: [describe your skills, experience and expertise]. I want to earn money beyond my current employment. Based on my background, what are the most realistic 5 ways I could generate additional income? For each, tell me: what it would involve, what I'd need to start, and what someone with my background could realistically charge.
✓ Found three options I hadn't considered. Started one of them within six weeks.
Common Questions

Everything
you wondered.

Is it wrong to use AI to help with salary negotiations?+
No more than it's wrong to practise what you're going to say, or to get advice from a mentor. AI helps you prepare and articulate. The conversation, the relationship, the decision — those are entirely yours.
What if I don't know what the market rate is?+
Ask AI. It can give you a solid starting range based on your role, location, and experience level. Use this as a starting point and do some additional research on sites like Glassdoor or LinkedIn Salary to confirm.
I'm scared to ask for a pay rise. How do I get over that?+
Preparation is the antidote to fear. The more you've prepared — the better you know your value, the more you've practised what to say, the more you've thought through the objections — the calmer you'll feel. Use the prompts here to over-prepare.
Can this help me if I'm self-employed or freelance?+
Especially. Freelancers often undercharge because they're bad at articulating their value. The Value Mirror prompt alone is worth the time of anyone who sets their own rates.
What if they say no?+
Have a plan B prepared. Ask AI: 'They said no to a pay rise citing budget constraints. What should I say next and what should I negotiate for instead?' There is almost always something to negotiate — timing, a review date, additional benefits, extra holiday.
