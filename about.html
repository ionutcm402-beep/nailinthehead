From one ordinary person

NOT A PROGRAMMER.

I do not work in technology. I do not have a computer science degree. Everything you see here was built with the help of AI — and that is exactly why this website exists.

Keep scrolling
Where I started

I heard about AI
everywhere.
And understood
nothing.

Like most people, I started hearing about AI everywhere. On the news. In conversations at work. In articles that made it sound either world-ending or world-saving, depending on which one you read.

Some people were excited. Some people were afraid. Most people — including me — were confused.

I had one question. Not: how does it work? Not: should I be worried? Not: how do I become an AI developer?

Just this: how can this technology help me live a better life?

That turned out to be the right question. And it was surprisingly hard to find anyone answering it plainly, honestly, for ordinary people.

So I started experimenting. One prompt at a time. One question at a time. One discovery at a time.

The biggest discovery

I built this.
All of it.
Without knowing
how to code.

Every room. Every prompt. Every tool. Every article in the journal. The homepage, the navigation, the animations — all of it. Built by someone who works in a completely different field and had never written a line of code in their life.

I am not saying this to impress you. I am saying it because it matters to the point this website is making.

If AI could help me build this, it can help you do whatever the equivalent thing is for you.

The gap between "I want to make something" and "I know how to make something" has never been smaller. The tools that used to belong to specialists now belong to anyone willing to ask good questions.

This website is not just a guide to using AI. It is proof that the guide works.

"
A few years ago, building something like this would have been impossible for me. Not because I lacked the idea — I had the idea. But because the gap between "I want to make this" and "I know how to make this" was wider than I could cross alone. AI closed that gap. Completely.
What this website is built with
Conversations with AI. Hundreds of questions. Dozens of iterations. Curiosity, patience, and the willingness to try things and see what happens.

No computer science degree. No coding bootcamp. No prior experience.

Just a person with an idea and a tool that could help them build it.
What I believe

Three things I keep
coming back to.

→
01
AI is not happening to us. It is happening for us.

Every major technology in history has been shaped more by how people used it than by what it was designed to do. The story of AI is not written yet. It is being written now — by the people who decide to engage with it, learn from it, and use it to build something better.

○
02
Ordinary people deserve extraordinary tools.

The best thinking partnerships have always been expensive — coaches, mentors, advisors. For the first time in history, that level of honest, patient, personalised help is available to everyone who knows how to ask. This is not a small thing. This is a fundamental change in what is possible.

◆
03
The question is not whether to use it. It is how.

I am not naive about the risks of this technology. I read about them. I think about them. But the people who will shape what AI becomes are the people who engage with it thoughtfully — not the people who stay on the sidelines. I would rather be someone learning to use a powerful tool well than someone watching others use it without me.

Why this exists

Not to teach
technology.
To change what
you believe
is possible.

I kept asking: where is the plain-English guide for people like me? For people who are not programmers, not tech enthusiasts, not early adopters — just people with a job, a life, and things they want to do better?

I couldn't find it. The resources that existed were either too technical, too hype-filled, or aimed at people who already understood the landscape.

Nobody was writing for the person who tried AI once, got a confusing answer, and gave up. Nobody was writing for the person who suspected it might be useful but couldn't figure out where to start. Nobody was making the case that AI is not just for developers and data scientists — it is for anyone who wants to learn faster, work smarter, earn more, create more, or think more clearly.

So I built what I wished had existed when I started.

Everything on this site — every prompt, every room, every article — is an answer to the question I kept asking: what should I say? Where should I start? How can this actually help me?

The mission is not to make you an AI expert. It is to help you discover what becomes possible when you learn how to use it.

A personal note

I want to say something directly, because I think it matters.

I am not an AI expert. I cannot explain how large language models work. I cannot tell you about parameters or training data or transformer architecture. There are brilliant people who can, and I am not one of them.

I am the person to follow if you want to know how to use it.

There is a difference — and it matters. The people who have changed their lives with AI are rarely the people who understand it technically. They are the people who learned what to ask. That is all this is. Learning what to ask.

I am still learning. Every week something surprises me. Every month there is a thing I understand now that I didn't understand before. This site will grow alongside that.

If you have found something useful here, please share it with one person you think it would help. That is how this grows.

And if you want to say hello — or if something here helped you — I would genuinely love to hear it.
